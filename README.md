                                   Sales & Finance Analytics – Coffee Shop Sales Dashboard  

                                            DOMAIN:- Sales & Marketing
                                 
                                                PROJECT OVERVIEW 
I developed a Sales Performance & Finance Dashboard to analyze customer behavior, sales patterns, and financial outcomes.  
This dashboard provides actionable insights into sales trends, product performance, and store-level revenue, helping decision-makers optimize resource allocation and business strategies.

                                                RECOMMENDED ANALYSIS

                                                  KEY INSIGHTS & RECOMMENDATION
   
                            1: How do sales vary by day of the week and hour of the day?
                                                        INSIGHT 1
Peak Hours = 9-10 AM Gold Mine
Order quantity spikes to 18-19K during 9-10 AM window, then crashes 50% by afternoon – massive untapped capacity waste.
Friday is the Profit Peak, Saturday is the Mystery Drop
Friday hits 101,373 orders but Saturday plummets to 96,894 despite weekend potential – losing 4,500+ orders weekly.

RECOMMENDATION

•	Launch "Happy Hour" pricing 2-5 PM (15% discount) to flatten demand curve
•	Introduce cold brew/iced coffee promotions for afternoon traffic
•	Staff optimization: shift 30% of morning crew to afternoon slots
•	Deploy Saturday-exclusive loyalty rewards (double points, free pastry with coffee)
•	Test extended Saturday hours (6 AM - 10 PM) to capture evening crowd
•	Partner with local events/markets for Saturday foot traffic

                             2: Are there any peak times for sales activity?
                                               INSIGHT  2
9-10 AM is the Dominant Revenue Window
Morning rush generates 18-19K orders (40%+ of daily volume) concentrated in single hour – operational bottleneck and opportunity.

RECOMMENDATION

•	Deploy mobile order-ahead app to reduce 9-10 AM queue times
•	Add express pickup station for pre-ordered items
•	Introduce "beat the rush" discount for 7-8 AM orders
•	Optimize register workflow: dedicated cash-only lane during peak

                          3: What is the total sales revenue for each month?
                                             INSIGHT 3
June = Peak Season, February = Revenue Valley
June generates $165K vs February's $98K – 68% variance indicates severe seasonal demand fluctuation or operational gaps in winter months.

RECOMMENDATION

•	Pre-buy inventory and staff up for May-July summer surge
•	Launch winter product line: hot chocolate bar, seasonal lattes, soup pairings for Feb-March
•	Test limited-time winter promotions: "Warm Up Wednesdays" with BOGO hot drinks
•	Analyze if February drop is weather-driven (delivery partnerships?) or menu fatigue.

                          4: How do sales vary across different store locations?
                                            INSIGHT 4
Lower Manhattan Underperforms Despite High Footfall
Astoria ($232K/$51K split) vs Lower Manhattan ($230K/$48K) – similar sales but Lower Manhattan has premium location economics going to waste with lower per-order value ($4.55 vs $4.90).

RECOMMENDATION

•	Audit Lower Manhattan: Are high rents eating profits? Is menu priced too low?
•	Test premium product mix (specialty lattes, nitro cold brew, avocado toast) for corporate crowd
•	Implement dynamic pricing: charge $0.50 more for drinks in financial district
•	If margins stay thin, consider relocating to cheaper Lower Manhattan micro-location or pop-up model

                             5: What is the average price/order per person?
                                            INSIGHT 5
$4.70 Average Order = Leaving Money on Table
Hell's Kitchen achieves $4.90/order vs Lower Manhattan's $4.55 – $0.35 gap across thousands of orders = significant lost revenue. Limited upselling and combo penetration.
Average Order is $4.70 – Room to Grow
With 1.44 items/order, customers aren't adding extras – massive ticket size expansion opportunity sitting untapped.

RECOMMENDATION

•	Train staff on upsell scripts: "Make it a large for just $0.75 more?"
•	Introduce combo meal defaults at POS: coffee automatically paired with pastry unless declined
•	Test tiered pricing: $4.99 "Quick Coffee", $6.99 "Breakfast Deal", $8.99 "Premium Experience"
•	Launch loyalty app with minimum $6 spend for points to drive ticket size
•	Gamify upsells: "You're $1.30 away from free delivery" (if app-based)
•	Bundle pricing: Coffee + muffin + juice = $8 (vs $9.50 individually)
•	Train baristas on suggestive selling: "Our hot chocolate pairs perfectly with that espresso"

                    6: Which products are the best-selling in terms of quantity and revenue?
                                          INSIGHT 6
Brewed Chai Tea is the Hidden Champion
$77,082 sales from chai (2nd place) vs $91,406 espresso – chai has 85% of top product's revenue with likely higher margins and untapped growth potential.

RECOMMENDATION

•	Expand chai variants: Masala Chai, Cardamom Chai, Seasonal flavors (Pumpkin Spice Chai)
•	Bundle chai + pastry combos at $6.50 (vs $4.70 avg order) to boost ticket size
•	Market chai as "artisan alternative" to coffee-fatigued customers via Instagram campaign
•	Train baristas on chai customization to create premium experience (oat milk upgrade, extra spice)

                           7: How do sales vary by product category and type?
                                          INSIGHT 7
Regular Size Dominates (31%) But Small is Ignored (7%)
Only 7% choose small despite likely being $1-2 cheaper – pricing structure broken or small size poorly positioned.
Coffee Category is 39% But Bakery is Just 12%
Coffee dominates but food attachment is weak – missing easy margin boost from pastries/sandwiches. Bakery should be 25%+ of mix.
Drinking Chocolate & Flavours Are Dead Weight (1% Each)
Niche categories dragging down menu complexity and inventory costs – killing operational efficiency.

RECOMMENDATION

Size Optimization:
•	Rebrand "small" as "espresso shot" or "quick sip" at $2.99 rush-hour special
•	Introduce "large" upsell prompts at POS with visible size comparison cups
•	Test removing "Not Defined" (28%) by forcing size selection – could shift 10-15% to large
Category Rebalancing:
•	Mandatory combo training: "Pair your coffee with a croissant for just $2 more"
•	Display fresh bakery items at eye level near register (not behind counter)
•	Launch breakfast sandwich subscription: 5 combos for $30 (vs $35 a la carte)
•	Add grab-and-go refrigerated section with yogurt parfaits, protein boxes for lunch crowd
Menu Simplification:
•	Kill bottom 3 categories (drinking chocolate, flavours), reinvest shelf space in top 5 performers
•	Rotate drinking chocolate to "winter seasonal special" only (Nov-Feb)
•	Simplify menu to 15-20 hero items for faster service and 30% lower waste.




                                                      KEY LEARNINGS 
1.  Mastered ETL processes for clean, reliable data.  
2. Built interactive dashboards with deep insights.  
3. Strengthened understanding of customer behavior via time-based analysis.  
4.Identified sales drivers by product category & store location.  
5. Enhanced reporting & visualization skills for business decision-making.  
6. DIG DEEPER THAN AVERAGES
Averages hide winners and losers – always split data by location, time, and product to find what's actually working.
7. FIX WHAT'S BROKEN FIRST
Biggest wins are simple fixes like moving staff to slow hours, displaying products better, and cutting menu bloat.
8. SMALL % = BIG MONEY
Low-selling items (1-2%) seem harmless but drain profits through inventory waste, slow service, and confused staff.
9. ATTACH DOLLAR SIGNS TO IDEAS
Vague suggestions get ignored – specific recommendations with revenue impact ($15K/month) get approved instantly.
10. PATTERNS REVEAL OPPORTUNITIES
Weird patterns (Friday strong but Saturday weak, chai selling 85% of espresso) show exactly where to focus energy.
